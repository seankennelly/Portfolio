This was my first single-page project, which presented new challenges.

With plenty of content, my goal was to keep it organised, uncluttered and interesting to look at.

To avoid large unbroken chunks of text I used:

This was the first site I launched, and as expected there were many obstacles to overcome.

I began building this site before I was aware of responsive design. Once it was completed it had to be scrapped and begun again.

Hosting the site presented numerous difficulties.

Transferring the domain to a new site and hosting service was complex, as the previous host was a small local company that had largely moved on from web hosting.

The website I had replaced was not secured and was badly built, so was inaccessible by search engines. Getting the new site to appear in search results provided many more unexpected learning opportunities and taught me a lot about SEO.

The client and I went through the previous site, saving and rejecting content for the new site. My designs conformed to their company branding.

Unfortunately they were unable to provide updated photos, so the layout was restricted by small images that could not be sized too large.

Ivybridge Walking Club

Build (using a site-creation platform), Administration, Handover

I was hired was to overhaul this site then hand it back to the club to update and maintain it. For this purpose I created a detailed 80-page process manual and delivered a training session.

Info

Page

Back

To keep costs down for the club I built the site using a free site-building platform.

The challenge of this job was ensuring the club could maintain the website once I had handed it over to them, as the club's board are of a generation that is not confident with technology.

To assist them I created a detailed process manual to follow. The processes are broken down into clear instructions, with screenshots and highlights for ease of use. I made no assumptions about the user's abilities or foreknowledge of common UI standards.

The board was initially intimidated by the process manual, so I delivered a training session for them. I discussed the manual's structure and uses, demonstrated processes, and answered all questions put to me by the board.

This job had very positive outcomes. The board were very happy with the training session and manual, and I was able to successfully hand over the running of the site to them.
